Prerequisites: Calc II (Chapters 5-8 in Stewart) and Calc III (Chapters 10, 12-14 in Stewart).

Course Overview: We will begin by extending the theory of integration to functions of two or three variables, making use of cylindrical and spherical coordinates.  Along the way, we'll apply multiple integrals to calculate volume, center of mass, moments of intertia, electric charge, and probability.  Next, we'll use vector geometry to compute line and surface integrals.   Then we'll explore three fundamental theorems (Green, Stokes, Divergence) that relate differentiation and integration of vector fields.  We'll finish with a look at applications of these theorems to electrostatics and hydrodynamics.

Homework: Assignments and due dates will be updated in the (tentative) schedule below.  To the right of each lecture you'll find an assignment from the corresponding section of the textbook.  I can't emphasize enough how important it is that you complete the day's assignment before the next class.  Officially, however, the assignments will be due in batches on Mondays and Thursdays in order to accomodate any outside commitments that some of you may have on certain days of the week, as well as emergencies.  All assingments must be turned in to my mailbox near Math 406 before class on the day they are due.  I'll collect them from my mailbox at exactly 1pm (according to the clock on my cell phone).  Late work will receive NO credit, so I strongly encourage you to turn in your assignments early.  Please, no mad dashes up the stairs!  Note: A few assignments are due on Tuesdays.

Here's my recommended approach to the homework.  First, look over your lecture notes and read through the section in Stewart until the examples make sense to you.  Next, attack the problems in order (as they tend to get harder), looking back if you get stuck.  For those problems that still have you stumped, don't hesitate to work together with a classmate...two heads are often better, and more fun, than one.  Just be sure to write up the solutions on your own.  No copied work.  Also, many assigned questions will have answers in the back of the book, so I encourage you to check your final answers and correct any errors before you submit them!  Of course, no credit will be given for an answer without clear justification.  So show your work and draw lots of diagrams as this is a very visual subject!

Homework Solutions: Shortly after assignments are turned in, solutions will posted here.  I'll tell you the password in class.

Quizzes and Exams:  There will be three 20 minute quizzes, a 90 minute midterm, and a 3 hour final exam.  Quiz 1 will be on the integration techniques in Chapter 7 that you should have learned in Calculus II but may need to review.  Quiz 2 will be on double integrals (15.1-15.5).   The midterm will cover all of Chapter 15.  Quiz 3 will be on vector calculus through Green's Thm (16.1-16.4).  As for the final, while all material is fair game, the vast majority of questions will concern Chapter 16 (especially the latter half) and applications.

Calculators: Calculators will not be permitted on exams or quizzes.  They may be used sparingly on homework to evaluate numerical data or check/motivate solutions, but you won't receive credit for an antiderivative done on a calculator or computer without written justification.  To help with visualization, I encourage you to use Grapher (standard on a Mac), Mathematica, Matlab, Maple, or any high-powered calculator to graph any interesting functions and equations that arise in the homework!
